What Is Connect6?

Connect6 is a two-player strategy game played on a 19x19 board. Black places one stone on the opening move, then both players take turns placing two stones. The first to form an unbroken line of six stones — horizontally, vertically, or diagonally — wins.

The double-move rule is the key mechanic. After Black's opening stone, every turn adds two stones, which keeps the count difference at exactly one and gives the second player consistent counterattack options. No forbidden-move rules are needed.

How to Play Connect6

Black places one stone on the first turn. From the second turn onward, both players place two stones per turn. Click an empty intersection for the first stone, then click again for the second. The AI responds on its turn with two stones. Connect six in a row to win.

Use Undo to take back the most recent pair of stones (yours and the AI's). Use New Game to clear the board at any time.

How Connect6 Differs From Gomoku

Connect6 shares its core with Gomoku — line up stones on a board — but the double-move rule changes the dynamics. Stone counts stay close throughout the game, and the second player has more opportunities to respond to threats. The result is a slower, more positional game than standard Gomoku.

Frequently Asked Questions
How does the AI work?▼
Easy picks moves with light evaluation. Medium balances attack and defense with a heuristic tuned to the double-move rule. Hard searches deeper and recognizes double threats.
Why does Connect6 use two stones per turn after the first move?▼
The double-move rule keeps the stone count difference at one after every turn, which gives the second player consistent counterattack options. It avoids the need for forbidden-move rules used in standard Gomoku.
Can I undo multiple moves?▼
Yes. Each Undo removes the most recent pair of stones (yours and the AI's). Click repeatedly to rewind further.
Is Connect6 more complex than Gomoku?▼
Yes. The double-move rule produces more branching positions per turn, which means deeper calculation is needed. Many players treat Connect6 as a step up from Gomoku in required reading depth.
What happens if the board fills up without a winner?▼
The game ends in a draw. No points are awarded. Draws are uncommon and typically occur only when both sides defend every threat.

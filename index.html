What Is Gomoku?

Gomoku, also called Five in a Row, is one of those games that takes a minute to learn and a lifetime to get good at. Two players take turns placing black and white stones on a 15x15 grid. The first to line up five stones in a row — horizontally, vertically, or diagonally — wins.

The rules are dead simple, but the strategy runs deep. That's what keeps people coming back. This version lets you play against a decent AI opponent right in your browser, no downloads or accounts needed.

How to Play

Black goes first. Click any empty intersection to place your stone. The AI will respond immediately. First to five in a row wins.

Made a mistake? Hit the Undo button to take back your last move (and the AI's response). Want a clean slate? Click New Game. You can also switch difficulty anytime — Easy for a relaxed game, Hard if you want a real challenge.

Key Strategies

  • Four-Three Fork: Set up a position where you threaten two winning lines at once. Your opponent can only block one.
  • Live Three: Three stones in a row with both ends open. Block it immediately or you will lose.
  • Control the Center: Early stones near the middle give you more directions to build threats.
  • Think Ahead: Try to read two or three moves ahead. Gomoku is all about forcing your opponent into impossible choices.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does the AI work?+
Easy picks mostly random moves with basic defense. Medium balances attack and defense. Hard looks two moves ahead and actively hunts for forks. You will notice the difference.
Can I undo multiple moves?+
Yes. Each undo removes your last move and the AI's response. Keep clicking to rewind as far as you want.
Does changing difficulty reset my score?+
No, your win/loss count stays the same. Hit the reset button next to the score if you want to start fresh.
Why does Hard AI take longer?+
It is doing more calculation — checking threats, evaluating positions, looking for forced wins. We also added a small delay so it feels more natural, not robotic.
What happens if the board fills up?+
The game ends in a draw. No points awarded. It is rare, but it happens when both players defend perfectly.

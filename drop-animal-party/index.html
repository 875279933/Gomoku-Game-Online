The game

What is Drop Animal Party?

Drop Animal Party is a merge puzzle you play in your browser. Cute animals fall from the top of the board. You aim each one with the mouse or your finger. When two of the same kind bump into each other, they pop into a bigger animal and your score jumps.

There's a dashed line near the top of the box. If your animals stack past it for more than a second, the round ends. Aim well, merge often, beat your best. A full run takes about three minutes, which is why people come back to Drop Animal Party between meetings or on the train.

Getting started

How Drop Animal Party works

Five steps. The first round teaches you the rest.

  1. Move your mouse or finger to set the drop angle at the top of the board.
  2. Press Drop, click the board, or hit Space to release the animal.
  3. When two of the same kind touch, they merge into the next size up.
  4. Bigger merges are worth more points. Chain reactions score the highest.
  5. The round ends if animals stack past the dashed line for more than a second.

Strategy

Tips for higher scores

Drop Animal Party rewards patience more than speed. Slow down for a moment and the board opens up.

  • Build from the bottom up. Empty gaps under a small animal are painful to fix later.
  • Keep your biggest animals in the middle so they don't roll into the walls.
  • Drop new animals next to a matching pair to chain reactions.
  • Watch the next-up animal in the corner. Skip drops that don't fit your plan.
  • Avoid narrow gaps. A pinned animal will trap you in two moves.
Questions

Drop Animal Party FAQ

Is Drop Animal Party free?

Yes. No sign-up, no install, no ads inside the game.

How is my best score saved?

It lives in your browser's local storage on this device. Clearing site data clears the score.

Can I play on my phone?

Yep. Touch and swipe work on any modern mobile browser, and the board scales to fit.

What's the largest animal?

The dragon. Two dragons don't merge, so use them as a backstop instead of a target.

Why did the game end so fast?

Probably a tall stack near the top. Aim for flat piles early. Tall piles leak past the dashed line and end the run.

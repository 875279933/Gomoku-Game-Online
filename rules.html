Official Gomoku Rules

Effective Date: April 28, 2026

Gomoku, also known as Five in a Row, is a classic abstract strategy game played worldwide. Whether you challenge our AI or play with a friend, these rules define fair and exciting matches on Play Gomoku Online.

📖 The Game & Objective

Gomoku is a game played between two players, Black and White, on a board. Players take turns placing black and white stones, and the first player to form a row of five stones horizontally, vertically, or diagonally wins.

The goal is to connect five stones in a row while preventing your opponent from doing the same. Every move influences both offense and defense.

⚙️ How to Play (Basic Rules)

  • Turn order: Black moves first, followed by White. Players alternate turns until the game ends.
  • Placing stones: On your turn, place one stone of your color on any empty intersection of the board.
  • No undo / no removal: Once a move is made, it cannot be undone or moved. Think carefully before you play.
  • Standard board: While the classic Gomoku board is 15×15, our online version uses a clean 15×15 grid that ensures fair competitive play.

No player may skip a turn, and each move must be made on an empty cell. The game proceeds until a winner is decided or the board fills completely.

🏆 Winning the Game

The first player to connect five stones in a row wins immediately. The winning line can be formed in any of three directions:

  • Horizontally (left to right on the same row)
  • Vertically (top to bottom on the same column)
  • Diagonally (any diagonal line with 45° slope)

Note: A line of exactly five stones wins the game. Lines longer than five (called overlines) do not change the win condition – the player who first achieves an unbroken chain of five consecutive stones claims victory.

🤝 Draw Condition

If the board is filled with stones and no line of five has been formed by either player, the game ends in a draw. It’s a rare but possible outcome of a perfectly balanced defensive battle.

🎯 Essential Strategy & Tips

Winning at Gomoku requires sharp pattern recognition and forward thinking. The best strategy is to block your opponent as soon as they have three stones in a row. Three stones form an “open three” which can quickly become four and then five on the next move. Strong players build multiple threats at once.

💡 Pro Tip: “The best strategy is to block your opponent as soon as they have three stones in a row.”

Create your own double threats (two lines of three or four) to force a win. Offense is defense – but never ignore your opponent's formation.

📋 Complete Rule Summary

  • How to Play: There are two colors, black and white. Black moves first, followed by white, with players taking turns. Once a move is made, it cannot be undone.
  • Winning: The first player to connect five stones in a row horizontally, vertically, or diagonally (a "line of five") wins.
  • Draw: If the board is filled with stones and no line of five has been formed, the game ends in a draw.
  • Fair play: No extra moves, no swapping stones. The game is decided purely by skill and strategy.

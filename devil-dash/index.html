The game

What is Devil Dash?

Devil Dash is a side-scrolling endless runner built for the browser. You play a small devil with a pitchfork, sprinting along a lava-lit track. Obstacles scroll in from the right side of the screen. Tap to jump over them, chain jumps for air time, and rack up points for every meter you survive.

The track never looks the same twice. Spikes, low walls, and floating flames appear in different orders. Speed ramps up the longer you stay alive, so a calm first second often turns into a frantic last second. A full run of Devil Dash usually lasts between thirty seconds and three minutes, depending on how brave you feel.

Getting started

How Devil Dash works

Five steps. The first jump teaches you the rest.

  1. Pick a difficulty: Easy, Normal, or Hard. Each level changes the start speed, the top speed, and how fast traps roll in.
  2. The devil starts running the moment the round begins. No warm-up.
  3. Press Space, click the board, or tap the screen to jump over a trap.
  4. Chain two jumps in a row to clear taller obstacles. Don't tap too fast or you'll land early.
  5. Each meter adds to your score. Touch a spike or a wall and the run ends.

Shortcuts: 1 = Easy · 2 = Normal · 3 = Hard · P = Pause · R = Reset

Strategy

Tips for longer Devil Dash runs

Devil Dash punishes panic. Slow eyes beat fast fingers.

Read the next two obstacles

Don't look at the spike under your feet. Look at the trap two meters ahead and pre-load your jump.

Hold the jump for high walls

A tap gives a short hop. A held button gives a tall arc. Tall walls need the tall arc.

Stay near the middle

The left and right edges crowd the screen and shrink your reaction window. Center yourself.

Don't chase the soul orbs

Orbs add a small bonus. They're not worth a death. Skip them when a trap is closing in.

Questions

Devil Dash FAQ

Is Devil Dash free?

Yes. No sign-up, no install, no paywalls. Open the page and play.

Where is my best score saved?

In your browser's local storage on this device. Clear site data and the score clears too.

Does Devil Dash work on phones?

Yes. Tap the board to jump. The canvas scales down so the track stays readable on small screens.

What's the highest possible score?

The game has no cap. Speed keeps climbing, so eventually the gap between traps gets shorter than your jump time and the run ends.

Can I play with one hand?

Yes. Thumb on the screen, jump with the same thumb. The whole game runs off a single input.

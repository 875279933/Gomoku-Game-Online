How to Win in Gomoku

Gomoku is a globally popular two-player board game. While the rules can be learned in just 10 seconds, consistently winning requires mastering real strategy. Whether you're playing against friends on Game Pigeon or competing in online tournaments, this guide will help you understand how to win in Gomoku more consistently.

Learning how to win in Gomoku isn't just about luck—it's about understanding patterns, controlling the board, and outthinking your opponent. By applying these core strategies, you'll significantly improve your chances to win in Gomoku every time you play.

8 Core Tips for How to Win in Gomoku

1 Secure the Center in the Opening

The center of the board is the most valuable area when learning how to win in Gomoku. From the center, you can expand in four directions simultaneously: horizontally, vertically, diagonally left, and diagonally right. Try to place your first three moves in the central area.

Placing a piece near the edge significantly reduces the directions in which you can expand, whereas every piece in the center retains all possible angles of attack. This foundational strategy is essential if you want to know how to win in Gomoku.

2 Create a "Live Four" — The Ultimate Game-Ending Move

A "live four" consists of four consecutive stones with neither end blocked by the opponent. Knowing how to create and recognize a live four is crucial to learning how to win in Gomoku.

Once a live four is formed, you can win immediately by placing a stone at either end. Your opponent can block only one end at most, leaving the other end open for your next move. If you see your opponent forming a live four, you must block it immediately; otherwise, you will lose the next turn. This is a critical defensive move when learning how to win in Gomoku.

3 Two-Pronged Attack (Double Threat) — The Most Reliable Way to Win

Instead of building a single straight line, create two winning threats simultaneously. This is one of the most effective tactics to know when learning how to win in Gomoku. Your opponent can only block one in a single turn, while the other will secure your victory.

Example: First, form a three-in-a-row. Don't rush to add the fourth stone; instead, jump one space to the side to create two separate live threes. Common double-threat shapes include the "L-shape," "big two-jump," and "zigzag" formations. Mastering these patterns is key to understanding how to win in Gomoku consistently.

4 Using "Live Threes" to Pressure Your Opponent

Live Three: Three stones with both ends unblocked. Learning how to use live threes effectively is essential to how to win in Gomoku.

Once you form a live three, your opponent must immediately defend one end, effectively costing them a turn. Here's a sequential attack template that demonstrates how to win in Gomoku using live threes:

Live three → Opponent blocks → Live three in the other direction → Opponent blocks again → Create a live four → Win.

This allows you to gradually force your opponent into a dead end, which is a key strategy for how to win in Gomoku.

5 When Should You Defend? How to Defend Effectively?

Defense is just as important as offense when learning how to win in Gomoku. The table below provides clear defense priorities:

Opponent's Position What You Should Do
Live four You've already lost (must be stopped before it appears)
Four-in-a-row (with one end blocked) Immediately block the only end that leads to victory
Three-in-a-row Block one end to prevent it from becoming four-in-a-row
Double threat Find the intersection of the two threat lines and place a stone to disrupt both attack lines simultaneously

Advanced defense technique: When multiple defense points are available, prioritize a position that also helps you develop your own attack. This allows you to accomplish two things in a single turn—a key principle for how to win in Gomoku.

6 Always Aim for a "Winning Sequence"

Before every move, ask yourself: "If I play here, do I already have a winning sequence?" This mindset is essential when learning how to win in Gomoku.

VCF (Victory by Four-in-a-Row): Play a four-in-a-row (with one end blocked but four stones) on every move. Your opponent will be forced to block, and eventually, you'll create a live four-in-a-row to win immediately. This is the standard technique used by high-level players to end the game and a critical part of knowing how to win in Gomoku.

7 Set Traps & Psychological Warfare

Open-line feint, hidden-line kill: Intentionally set up a line that appears to be winning, luring your opponent into defending that line with all their strength, while you quietly build a true winning line elsewhere. This deceptive tactic can be highly effective when learning how to win in Gomoku.

Pacing the Game: When your consecutive live threes are perfectly blocked by your opponent, consider playing a "neutral move"—one that appears unrelated but actually connects two regions, setting the stage for a double-kill. Mastering these mind games is another layer of how to win in Gomoku.

8 Learn and Master Black's Advantageous Openings

If you play as Black, memorize these classic openings, which establish a strong advantage in the opening moves. Knowing these openings is a key part of how to win in Gomoku when playing as Black.

Opening Name Features Difficulty
Flower Moon Creates a double-live-three threat on the third move Simple, suitable for beginners
Cold Star Combines straight and diagonal lines, very solid Basic, high reliability
Stream Moon Classic offensive opening Versatile and effective

The core idea behind these openings is to establish multi-directional connections early on, leaving White unable to defend against all threats. This strategic approach is fundamental to how to win in Gomoku as Black.

💡 Pro Tip: To truly master how to win in Gomoku, practice these strategies regularly. Start with simpler tactics like controlling the center, then gradually incorporate double threats and opening theory into your game.

What Is Gomoku?

Gomoku, also known as Five in a Row, is a classic strategy board game played on a 15 by 15 grid. Two players take turns placing black and white stones on empty intersections, and the first to line up five stones horizontally, vertically, or diagonally wins. Our Gomoku AI brings this timeless game straight to your browser with a sharp computer opponent that adapts to your style.

The rules take seconds to learn, yet the strategy runs surprisingly deep. This Gomoku AI engine evaluates threats, hunts for forks, and looks ahead so every match feels like a genuine contest. No downloads, no accounts — just open the page and start playing.

Winning Strategies

Want to beat the AI more often? These core tactics will sharpen your play.

The Four-Three Fork

Set up a position where you threaten two winning lines at once. Your opponent can only block one, which means you win on the next turn.

Live Three Defense

Three stones in a row with both ends open is called a live three. Let it survive and you are probably done. Block it the moment it appears.

Control the Center

Early stones near the middle of the board give you more directions to build threats. The edges limit your options, so claim central space whenever you can.

Think Two Moves Ahead

Try to read a couple of turns forward. Gomoku is about forcing impossible choices, so set up sequences where every reply still leaves you ahead.
